üè≠ How Humans Affect Animals

Animals live all around us! They live in forests, oceans, rivers, deserts, and even in our cities. But sometimes, the things people do can change where animals live and how they live.

When we build houses, roads, and shopping centers, we might cut down trees or fill in ponds. This is called changing an animal's habitat. A habitat is the place where an animal lives and finds food, water, and shelter.

üå≥ What is a Habitat?

A habitat is like an animal's home and neighborhood all together! It has everything the animal needs to survive: food to eat, water to drink, places to hide from danger, and space to raise babies.

üòü How Do Humans Change Animal Habitats?

There are many ways that human activities can affect animals and their homes:

Ways Humans Impact Animals:

üèóÔ∏è Building and Construction: When we build new houses, schools, or roads, we might cut down forests or fill in wetlands. Animals that lived there need to find new homes.

üóëÔ∏è Pollution and Litter: Trash and chemicals can hurt animals. Plastic bags in the ocean can harm sea turtles who think they are jellyfish. Oil spills make birds' feathers dirty so they can't fly.

üé£ Overfishing and Hunting: Taking too many fish from the ocean or hunting too many animals means there aren't enough left. This can make some animals very rare.

üå°Ô∏è Climate Change: When we drive cars and use lots of electricity, it can make the Earth warmer. This melts ice where polar bears live and makes it harder for them to find food.

üîä Noise and Light: Loud sounds from cars and bright lights at night can confuse animals. Birds might fly into lit-up buildings, and whales can get confused by ship noises.

üêª Real Examples of Impact

üêù

Bees: When people use chemicals on farms, it can hurt bees. Bees are important because they help flowers grow!

üê¢

Sea Turtles: Beach lights confuse baby turtles trying to find the ocean. They get lost and can't reach the water safely.

ü¶é

Salamanders: When roads are built through forests, salamanders can't safely cross to find water. Many get hurt trying to cross.
